About the Artist

Growing up in the idyllic village of Ardmore county Waterford, Padraic has been surrounded with stunning views of the sea and historic village life which has greatly influenced his work. Having developed a love of painting the scenes of his native Ardmore, Padraic then attended the National College of Art, Dublin where he completed his degree in textile art. There he developed his own personal style of painting which is unique to him. For many years he has contributed his work to the Ardmore Pottery which is a regular stockist of his work. Padraic currently practices in the very lovely countryside of North Tipperary, near to the mythical Knockshigowna Hill and the historic Georgian town of Birr. Padraics commissions are most notably of landscapes, streetscapes and vernacular architecture.

Latest News

Since moving to north Tipperary, Padraic has enjoyed exploring the local landscapes, peatlands and Georgian historic buildings, which now feature in his latest work. Most recently, Padraic completed an exhibition which was featured in Birr Library in 2021, depicting the historic streets of Birr and it's Georgian streetscapes. These are now beautifully framed and available for purchase.
